Japanese Language Journey

the story of my journey learning Japanese alone

This is me, I love video games and technology. I like to draw but I am not good at it. I enjoy baking.

I like playing on all platforms of gaming, someone please sell me a ps5 so I can play the new god of war....

I like anime too, FLCL is one of the anime that resonated with me the most.

I began to have an interest in learning Japanese when I had first realized I was gated off to content that I found interesting. I enjoyed a bunch of japanese media such as manga and video games, there are a ton of japanese content that never gets translated or ported over or even talked about in the united states where I lived so I took it upon myself to start learning japanese on my own.

In the beginning I tried to enroll in classes but there was no classes offering japanese around me beyond a university where id have to enroll for 4yrs minimum in a field I did not plan to have a career in. It was hard to come to terms with but there was no one who would get me started in learning that is reasonable, my options were transfers universities for a single class, move to japan as an english teacher or start learning on my own. So obviously there was only one choice.

I started with learning the basics, hiragana and katakana. As most english speakers who try to learn japanese on their own I wanted to make it as easy as possible looking up alphabet songs in japanese to get all the letters down but that doesnt exist considering to learn japanese you have to learn a new way of thinking and that kind of thing doesnt work in the japanese language as it does in english. I started off writing and speaking all the characters every day and using a website called realkana to learn the basic characters.
